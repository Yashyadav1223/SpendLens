# GTM

The exact target user is a technical founder, CTO, head of engineering, or engineering manager at a seed to Series B startup with 5-80 employees. They are using multiple AI tools across the team: Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, OpenAI API, Anthropic API, Gemini, and possibly v0 or Windsurf. They are not yet large enough to have a procurement team, but their monthly AI bill is starting to feel messy and hard to explain.

Right before they want Spendlens, they are probably doing one of three things: reviewing a company credit card statement, preparing a burn update, or trying to decide whether to renew a team plan. They might Google phrases like "Cursor team pricing worth it," "ChatGPT Team vs Claude Team," "reduce OpenAI API cost," or "startup AI credits." They might also scroll X after seeing founders complain that AI tools are becoming a meaningful line item.

The best early distribution channels are founder and builder communities where spend transparency is already normal: X founder circles, Indie Hackers, r/SaaS, r/startups, YC founder Slack-style communities, AI engineering Discords, Cursor/Claude/Copilot discussions, and LinkedIn posts aimed at CTOs. I would also post teardown-style content: "We audited a 12-person startup's AI stack and found $1,642/month in avoidable spend." That is more compelling than generic SEO content.

To get the first 100 users in 30 days with $0 paid budget, I would start with direct outreach to founders and engineering leads, offering a free AI spend audit in exchange for feedback. Then I would post anonymized audit breakdowns publicly: common waste patterns, plan mismatch examples, and API routing opportunities. I would share a launch thread, submit to Product Hunt once the flow is polished, and ask each audited founder to share their public report link if it produced a useful insight.

The unfair distribution channel is Credex's own access to discounted AI infrastructure credits. A normal spend calculator can only say "you might be overspending." Credex can say "you might be overspending, and we may be able to unlock discounted credits." That makes the tool both useful and commercially relevant.

Week-1 traction if this works: 100 landing page visitors, 35 audit starts, 20 completed audits, 8 email captures, 3 Credex consultation requests, and at least 1 qualified lead with more than $500/month in estimated savings.
