# Economics

Spendlens is valuable to Credex if it creates qualified leads for discounted AI infrastructure credits. A converted lead is likely worth hundreds to thousands of dollars depending on the credit volume. For a rough estimate, assume a startup spends $5,000/month on AI tools and infrastructure. If Credex helps them move even 30% of that spend into discounted credits or negotiated supply, that is $1,500/month of influenced spend. If Credex earns a 10-20% gross margin or equivalent spread, that lead could be worth $150-$300/month in gross profit, or $1,800-$3,600 annually. For larger API-heavy teams, the value can be much higher.

CAC should be low at the beginning because the tool itself is the acquisition asset. Direct founder outreach has mostly time cost. If one hour of outreach produces five conversations and one completed audit, the "cash CAC" is near zero but the labor CAC is real. Community posts on X, Indie Hackers, Reddit, and founder groups also have near-zero cash CAC, but quality depends on the specificity of the post. Product Hunt could produce many low-intent users, but even a small number of high-savings audits can justify it.

A simple funnel could look like this:

- 1,000 visitors
- 35% start audit = 350 starts
- 60% complete audit = 210 completed audits
- 35% email capture after value = 74 leads
- 20% book Credex consultation = 15 calls
- 25% become credit customers = 4 customers

If each customer is worth $2,400/year in gross profit, that cohort creates $9,600 in annual gross profit. If content and founder outreach produce that with almost no paid spend, the economics are attractive.

For this tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months, Credex would need either higher lead volume or higher average converted account value. For example, 400 customers at $2,500 ARR, 200 customers at $5,000 ARR, or 100 customers at $10,000 ARR. That would require the tool to become a trusted benchmark, not just a calculator. The product would need strong shareable reports, credible pricing data, real benchmark comparisons, and a clear handoff from "you are overspending" to "Credex can help you capture the savings."

The most important economic assumption is the conversion rate from high-savings audit to consultation. If that rate is below 5%, the product is mostly educational. If it is 15-25%, Spendlens becomes a serious lead-generation channel.
