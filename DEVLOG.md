## Day 2 — 2026-05-11
**Hours worked:** 6.5
**What I did:** Built the frontend foundation for Spendlens, focused on the core acquisition and audit onboarding flow. I shifted the product from a generic dashboard into a B2B SaaS experience for AI infrastructure spend auditing, with messaging aimed at startup founders, CTOs, and engineering managers. I built a polished SaaS landing page with a sticky navbar, conversion-focused hero section, clear CTAs, feature sections, mock trusted-by logos, how-it-works content, pricing, FAQ, and a product-style dashboard preview. I also created the audit onboarding flow with a multi-step form where users can enter AI tools, selected plans, monthly spend, seat counts, usage type, team size, budget, and pain points. The form now persists progress in localStorage, includes a dynamic pricing summary sidebar, and is responsive across desktop and mobile. I also added animations, hover states, reusable UI components, and theme polish so the product feels closer to a real MVP instead of a static prototype.
**What I learned:** The biggest learning today was that SaaS onboarding needs to reduce friction while still collecting enough information to create value. For Spendlens, the landing page has to make the pain obvious quickly: teams are spending money across ChatGPT, Claude, Cursor, Copilot, Gemini, and APIs without a clear view of waste. The audit form then has to turn that pain into action by making spend input feel structured and manageable. I also learned that UI systems need semantic styling early, because hardcoded dark-mode colors create problems once light mode is introduced.
**Blockers / what I'm stuck on:** The main blocker is that the audit output is still mostly driven by dummy benchmark data instead of calculating recommendations directly from the user's form inputs. The product experience feels strong visually, but the next step is making the audit logic feel more personalized and credible. I also want to improve validation states so users understand exactly what is required before generating an audit.
**Plan for tomorrow:** Connect the onboarding form data more deeply to the audit results dashboard, build more realistic savings calculations, improve validation and empty states, and make the shareable report feel more like something a founder or CTO could send to a team member or investor. I also want to keep refining the mobile experience so the core audit flow works well even on smaller screens.
